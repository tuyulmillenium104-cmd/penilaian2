## What is Internet Court?

Internet Court is a decentralized dispute resolution framework designed to handle 
conflicts that emerge in digital environments. As economic activity increasingly 
moves online, especially onchain, traditional legal systems struggle to enforce 
agreements efficiently, globally, and in real time.

Internet Court proposes a system where disputes can be initiated, reviewed, and 
resolved using transparent procedures adapted to internet-native interactions.

It is built around the idea that the internet needs its own enforcement layer, 
one that is faster, borderless, and compatible with digital assets and smart contracts.

## The Core Problem

The modern internet economy operates across jurisdictions, pseudonymous identities, 
and autonomous smart contracts.

Traditional courts face structural limitations:
- They are geographically bound
- Legal processes are slow and expensive
- Cross-border enforcement is complex
- Smart contracts execute automatically, even when disputes arise
- Many digital agreements lack clear enforcement mechanisms

As more value moves onchain, dispute resolution becomes a critical missing primitive.

## Why This Matters in Web3

Smart contracts remove intermediaries, but they do not remove disputes.

Bugs, misaligned incentives, governance conflicts, oracle failures, fraud, and 
ambiguous agreements still happen. When they do, there is often no clear, efficient 
way to resolve them without resorting to centralized actors.

Internet Court aims to provide a structured, internet-native resolution layer that 
matches the speed and architecture of decentralized systems.

## Conceptual Shift

Internet Court represents a move from geographically bound legal enforcement to 
digitally native adjudication.

Instead of relying purely on nation-state courts, it explores how disputes between 
pseudonymous participants, DAOs, smart contract users, and global communities can 
be handled in a transparent and structured manner.

This is not about replacing legal systems, it is about creating complementary 
infrastructure for the internet economy.

## Why Now?

The growth of DAOs, DeFi protocols, NFT markets, digital freelance work, and 
AI-driven agents increases the probability of digital disputes.

As economic coordination becomes more automated and borderless, enforcement must 
evolve as well.

Internet Court positions itself as part of the foundational infrastructure needed 
for a fully functional digital economy.
